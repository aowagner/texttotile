## Text as the Foundation

TextToTile is built around structured plain text files that remain fully accessible outside the app itself. Unlike many planning and visualization tools that store information inside proprietary databases or closed systems, TextToTile works directly with ordinary text files that can be edited, processed, and maintained by both humans and AI tools.

This distinction is important: TextToTile’s role is not to control or generate data, but simply to present text-based structures and outlines in a clear visual form. Who — or what — maintains the underlying text on your computer is conceptually separate from the app itself.

Modern AI tools such as OpenAI Codex and Anthropic Claude Code already work directly with files and folders on local systems. In practical use, this could mean asking an AI to reorganize an outline, summarize sections, maintain structured metadata, generate new content, or update plans automatically inside the same files you already use in TextToTile.

Because the information remains as structured text, changes made by AI systems remain transparent and easy to inspect. TextToTile can then immediately visualize those updates as charts, dashboards, overviews, timelines, or planning structures that the user can monitor and adjust further.

This also opens the possibility of workflows where different AI agents maintain different parts of a larger system — for example research notes, project planning, schedules, writing structures, or structured datasets — while TextToTile acts as a stable visual layer for understanding and navigating the overall structure.

## Early Experiments

TextToTile is designed around the idea that humans and AI systems should collaborate through open, understandable text formats rather than closed application silos.

I have only begun experimenting with these AI workflows, but I plan to explore the possibilities further and share practical examples and experiences over time.
